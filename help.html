Quick Start

Here's the short version. If you need more detail, see the step-by-step guide below.

  1. Open the tool — Double-click index.html to open it in your web browser.
  2. Upload your painting — Drag your image file onto the dotted area, or click to browse for it.
  3. Enter your painting title and entry number — Type the title exactly as you want it to appear. Choose Entry #1 or Entry #2.
  4. Click "Prepare My Image" then "Download" — Your image is resized, renamed, and ready to submit.
Your images never leave your computer. All the processing happens right in your browser — nothing is uploaded to the internet.

Show Specifications

The tool produces images that meet the Florida Watercolor Society's juried show requirements by default. You don't need to remember these numbers — the tool takes care of them automatically.

Requirement Default
Longest side 1920 pixels
DPI (metadata flag) 72
File format JPEG (Baseline)
Color profile sRGB
Maximum file size 5 MB
If a specific show's prospectus lists different pixel or file-size requirements, click Advanced settings on the Painting Details step and enter the values from the prospectus. Leave any field blank to keep the default.

Detailed Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Open the Tool

Find the file called index.html on your computer and double-click it. It will open in your web browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox all work).

You'll see a page titled "FWS Image Prep" with a dotted upload area.

Step 2: Upload Your Painting Image

You have two ways to add your image:

  • Drag and drop — Find the image file on your computer and drag it onto the dotted area.
  • Click to browse — Click anywhere in the dotted area. A file picker will open so you can navigate to your image.

The tool accepts JPEG, PNG, and TIFF image files. After you upload, you'll see a small preview of your painting along with its current size.

Some browsers do not open every TIFF file reliably. If that happens, the tool will tell you and you can retry with a JPEG or PNG copy.

Click Next to continue.

Use the highest-quality image you have. The tool will resize it down, but it can't add detail to a small image.

Step 3: Enter Your Painting Details

Painting Title: Type the title of your painting exactly as you want it in the filename. For example: Sunset Over Tampa Bay

If your title contains characters that are not allowed in downloaded filenames, the tool removes them automatically while keeping the title as close as possible to what you typed.

A yellow note appears under the filename preview if your title includes any of &, ', ", /, \, or :. Some of those get stripped from the filename, and all of them occasionally cause hiccups on art-submission upload sites. Nothing stops you from continuing — it's a heads-up in case you'd rather reword the title.

Entry Number: FWS allows up to 2 entries per show. Click Entry #1 for your first painting or Entry #2 for your second.

Below the form, you'll see a preview of what the filename will look like, for example:

GALLIONcarol#1_Sunset Over Tampa Bay.jpg

Advanced settings (optional): Most shows use the default 1920 px / 5 MB / 72 DPI output, so you can ignore this panel. If a prospectus lists different requirements, click Advanced settings and enter the longest-side pixels, max file size, and/or DPI it asks for. Leave a field blank to keep the default for that field.

Step 4: Prepare and Download

Click the big "Prepare My Image" button. The tool will:

  • Resize your image to the right pixel dimensions
  • Convert it to JPEG format
  • Stamp the DPI metadata flag (72 by default)
  • Set the color profile to sRGB
  • Pick the highest JPEG quality that still fits under the max file size, so the image uses as much of the 5 MB budget as it can
  • Name it correctly for FWS

You'll see your original image side by side with the processed version, along with details about both.

Tip: click either image to expand it to full size. Scroll to zoom in and out, drag to pan around, and press Esc (or click the dark background) to close. This is useful for double-checking details on the processed image before you submit.

Click the green "Download" button to save the file to your computer. That's the file you'll upload to the FWS submission system.

If you have a second painting to submit, click "Prepare Another Image" and repeat the process. Remember to choose Entry #2 for your second painting.

How Filenames Work

The FWS requires a specific filename format. The tool creates this automatically:

GALLIONcarol#[number]_[painting title].jpg

Here's how the parts break down:

  • GALLION — Last name in uppercase
  • carol — First name in lowercase
  • #1 or #2 — Which entry this is
  • _ — Underscore separator
  • Painting title — What you typed, with filename-invalid characters removed automatically if needed
  • .jpg — File extension

Examples:

  • GALLIONcarol#1_Sunset Over Tampa Bay.jpg
  • GALLIONcarol#2_Morning Mist.jpg
  • GALLIONcarol#1_Sky Water Study.jpg

Common Questions

What image formats can I upload?

JPEG, PNG, and TIFF files. If your browser cannot open a TIFF, save or export a JPEG or PNG copy and use that instead.

Will this change my original image?

No. Your original file is never modified. The tool creates a brand new copy with the right settings. Your original stays exactly as it was.

Does this upload my images to the internet?

No. Everything happens entirely on your computer, inside your web browser. Your images are never sent anywhere. You don't even need an internet connection to use this tool.

What if my image is smaller than the required size?

FWS juried shows require at least 1800 pixels on the longest side. As soon as you upload an image shorter than that, the tool shows a yellow note under the preview with the actual pixel count and recommends re-scanning or re-photographing at a higher resolution before submitting. You can still continue — it's advisory, not a block.

The tool will not stretch or enlarge your image, because that would make it look blurry. For best results, start with the largest, highest-quality image you have.

What does DPI mean, and should I change it?

DPI stands for "dots per inch." It's a small flag the tool writes into the file's metadata — that's it. It does not change a single pixel of your image.

Print programs read the DPI flag to decide a physical print size: pixels ÷ DPI = inches. A 1920-pixel-wide image at 72 DPI would print about 27 inches wide; the same image at 300 DPI would print about 6.4 inches wide. The pixel content is identical either way.

Screens and web browsers ignore DPI entirely. An image looks the same on screen whether the DPI is 3, 72, or 300. So if you pick a low DPI in Advanced settings expecting the image to look worse, it won't — because DPI isn't a quality setting.

The tool stamps 72 DPI by default, which is fine for FWS digital submissions. Only change it if a specific show's prospectus asks for a different value (for example, 300 DPI for a print-related show). To actually change how the image looks on screen, change the "Longest side (pixels)" field in Advanced settings, not DPI.

What does "sRGB" mean?

sRGB is a standard way of representing colors that ensures your painting looks consistent on different screens. The tool converts your image to sRGB automatically.

Can I prepare images for someone other than Carol Gallion?

This version of the tool is set up specifically for Carol Gallion and creates filenames with her name. To use it for a different artist, the tool would need to be modified.

What browser should I use?

Any modern browser works: Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, or Apple Safari. If you're on a very old browser and something doesn't work, try updating your browser or switching to Chrome.

Can I use this on my iPad or tablet?

Yes. Open the file in your tablet's web browser. The layout adjusts to fit smaller screens. You'll tap instead of click, but everything works the same way.

Troubleshooting

I see a blank page when I open the file.

Make sure you're opening index.html, not one of the other files. Also make sure the file app.js is in the same folder as index.html — the tool needs both files to work.

The tool says "Please choose an image file."

The file you selected isn't recognized as an image. Make sure you're choosing a JPEG, PNG, or TIFF file. If your file has an unusual extension, try exporting or saving it as .jpg or .png first.

I picked a TIFF, but nothing happened.

If the browser cannot decode that TIFF file, the tool shows a message and clears the partial upload. Save the image as JPEG or PNG, then try again.

The download button doesn't seem to work.

Some browsers handle downloads differently. Try right-clicking (or long-pressing on a tablet) the Download button and choosing "Save link as..." or "Download linked file."

My processed image looks different from the original.

Small color shifts can happen when converting between color profiles. The processed image uses sRGB, which is the standard the FWS requires. The difference should be very subtle.

Where did my downloaded file go?

Check your Downloads folder. On most computers, that's where your browser saves files. Look for a file starting with "GALLIONcarol."

Something else went wrong.

Try refreshing the page (press F5 or the reload button in your browser) and start over. If the problem persists, try a different web browser.
